## 1. Project Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the requirements, structure, features, and design guidelines for the development of a business website for a laundry service based in Oreyo, Ikorodu, Lagos, Nigeria. The website will serve as the primary digital touchpoint for the business, enabling customers to learn about services, request pickups, and get in touch with the business.

### 1.3 Target Audience
- **Corporate/office workers** who need reliable, time-saving laundry care for work attire (shirts, suits, uniforms) and have limited time during the week.
- **Business owners** who need consistent, professional laundry service — potentially including bulk/recurring orders (e.g. for hospitality businesses, event spaces, or offices) and may value invoicing/business account options.
- Both segments value **convenience, trust, turnaround time, and professionalism** over the lowest possible price.

### 1.4 Website Goals
1. Establish credibility and a professional brand presence online.
2. Make it effortless for a busy professional to understand services and request a pickup.
3. Drive direct enquiries via phone call, WhatsApp, and a contact form.
4. Clearly communicate service area, pricing structure (or pricing on request), and turnaround times.
5. Be fully usable on mobile, since most Nigerian users will access via smartphone.
6. Protect customer data and business systems through security best practices from day one — not as an afterthought.

## 5. Design Guidelines

- **Tone:** Clean, professional, trustworthy — reflecting the corporate target audience. Avoid an overly casual or budget look.
- **Color palette suggestion:** Fresh, clean tones (whites, soft blues, or greens) paired with a strong accent color (e.g., navy or teal) to convey professionalism and cleanliness.
- **Typography:** Clear, modern sans-serif fonts for readability on mobile.
- **Imagery:** High-quality photos of clean folded/pressed clothing, delivery process, and (if possible) real facility/team photos rather than generic stock imagery.
- **Navigation:** Simple, no more than 6–7 top-level menu items; sticky header with call/WhatsApp button always visible.
- **Accessibility:** Sufficient color contrast, legible font sizes, alt text on images.

## 6. Technical Requirements

- **Platform:** Recommend a lightweight CMS (e.g., WordPress) or a custom-built responsive site, depending on budget and need for future self-editing. Whichever platform is chosen, it must have an active security track record and a maintained update cycle.
- **Hosting:** Reliable, reputable hosting with good uptime; consider a provider with servers/CDN performing well in Nigeria and a solid security/patching record.
- **Domain:** A local (.com.ng) or .com domain matching the business name, registered with WHOIS privacy protection where available.
- **Performance:** Optimized images and fast load times, important given variable mobile network speeds in Nigeria.
- **Forms:** All form submissions should send email and/or SMS notifications to the business (see Section 7 for how these must be secured).
- **Analytics:** Google Analytics (or similar) integrated to track visits and conversions, configured to respect visitor privacy.

## 7. Security Requirements

Security is treated as a **core requirement**, not an optional add-on, given that the site will collect customer contact details, pickup addresses, and corporate account information. The developer/agency must design and build with the following in mind:

### 7.1 Data Protection & Privacy
- Encrypt all data in transit using **HTTPS/SSL** across every page (not just the contact/forms pages), with automatic redirect from HTTP to HTTPS.
- Collect only the customer data actually needed (name, phone, email, address, service details) — avoid storing sensitive data unnecessarily.
- Publish a clear **Privacy Policy** page explaining what data is collected, why, and how it is stored/used, in line with Nigeria's **Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023**.
- Store any customer/order data in a properly secured database (access-controlled, encrypted at rest where the hosting platform supports it) rather than in plain text files or unprotected spreadsheets.

### 7.2 Form & Input Security
- Protect all forms (contact, pickup request, corporate account request) against spam and abuse using CAPTCHA or an equivalent bot-protection mechanism.
- Validate and sanitize all user input on both the client and server side to prevent injection attacks (e.g., SQL injection, cross-site scripting).
- Rate-limit form submissions to prevent automated abuse of enquiry/notification systems.

### 7.3 Platform & Infrastructure Security
- Keep the CMS, plugins/themes, and any third-party libraries **up to date** with a defined patching schedule — outdated plugins are a leading cause of small-business site breaches in Nigeria and globally.
- Use strong, unique credentials for all admin/CMS accounts, with **two-factor authentication (2FA)** enabled for admin access.
- Limit admin/backend access to only those who need it, with role-based permissions.
- Install a **web application firewall (WAF)** and/or use a hosting provider/CDN (e.g., Cloudflare) that offers DDoS protection and malware scanning.
- Schedule **regular automated backups** of the site and database, stored securely off-site, with a documented restore process.

### 7.4 Ongoing Monitoring & Maintenance
- Set up uptime and security monitoring/alerts so the business is notified quickly of outages or suspicious activity.
- Conduct a basic security review/audit before launch (checking for exposed admin paths, default credentials, misconfigured permissions, etc.).
- Agree on a maintenance plan post-launch (who applies updates and patches, and how often).

### 7.5 Business Communication Security
- Ensure the phone/WhatsApp number (**+234 703 483 8576**) and any email used for enquiries are protected from being harvested by spam bots (e.g., avoid displaying raw email addresses in plain HTML text where possible; use click-to-reveal or contact-form routing).
- If online payment is introduced in a future phase, it must use a reputable, **PCI-DSS-compliant payment gateway** (e.g., Paystack or Flutterwave) — the business should never handle or store raw card details directly.
